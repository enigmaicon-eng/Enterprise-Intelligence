## Definition
Strategic intelligence is the compounding capacity to make better bets over time — through explicit thesis formation, disciplined signal reading, and systematic learning from closed bets. It is the difference between a strategy that repeats itself year after year and one that gets progressively better calibrated to how the world actually works.

## Why It Matters
Most strategic processes have no compounding mechanism. Goals are set, reviewed, reset. The learning from what worked or didn't is anecdotal and stays with the person who ran the work. Strategic intelligence is the system that captures that learning, makes it explicit, and feeds it into the next cycle.

## Key Principles

- **Bets compound through postmortems, not through planning.** The planning artifact (the bet template) is the input; the postmortem is where the intelligence is generated. Skip the postmortem and the bet produces no durable learning.
- **Kill conditions are the integrity test.** A bet without a specific kill condition can't be falsified. Unfalsifiable bets reveal a preference for feeling strategic over being strategic.
- **Disconfirming evidence is the signal.** Confirming evidence is cheap — the world will produce it for almost any thesis if you look selectively. Disconfirming evidence is the hard signal. A review process that finds only confirming evidence has a filter problem, not a strategy problem.
- **Horizon balance prevents strategic myopia.** An all-H1 portfolio is executing without options. It wins in stable environments and breaks in volatile ones. An options position (H3 bets) is not optional — it's the hedge against being wrong about the environment.
- **The "what were we wrong about" section is the whole point.** A strategy review that produces no honest mistakes isn't a review — it's a narrative. The moment strategic intelligence compounds is when the honest mistake generates a pattern that changes the next bet.

## Confirmed Patterns

### Kill Condition Specificity
Bets with specific, observable kill conditions close cleanly and generate cleaner postmortems. Bets with vague kill conditions accumulate drag — they can't be cleanly killed, so they stay open past their informational value, consuming attention without compounding.

### Thesis Separation from Outcome
The thesis must be written at creation and read verbatim at review. Retroactively adjusting the thesis to match what happened destroys the learning signal. The only valid reason to change the thesis during the bet is if new information changes the fundamental belief — not if execution is going poorly.

### Signal Quality Over Signal Quantity
One reliable signal (a metric that directly measures what the thesis claims) is worth more than five noisy proxies. The OKR signal quality check exists for this reason: "does this KR actually measure the outcome the objective is pursuing?"

## Failure Modes
- **Retrospective thesis**: Rewriting the thesis to match outcomes after the fact. Destroys calibration.
- **Review without verdict**: A strategy review that produces observations but no Continue/Adjust/Double-down/Kill decisions. Observations without verdicts are not reviews.
- **Horizon collapse**: The portfolio drifts to all-H1 as bets age out of H2/H3 without being replaced. Results in a portfolio with no options and high concentration risk.
- **Postmortem avoidance**: Closed bets that don't get postmortems. The learning is permanently lost — not delayed, lost. It exists only in memory.
