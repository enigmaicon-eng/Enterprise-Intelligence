## Definition
Annual themes are the 3 strategic priorities shaping a given year — not goals (those are OKRs), not bets (those are in `strategy/active-bets.md`), but the thematic orientation that gives coherence to the bet portfolio. Themes answer: "What is this year about?" Bets answer: "What specific commitments express that theme?"

## Theme Design Rules

Themes must satisfy three tests:

1. **Trade-off visible**: A good theme implies what you're NOT doing. "Be excellent at everything" is not a theme. "Compound depth over breadth in technical intelligence" implies a choice against shallow-and-broad.
2. **Actionable framing**: Themes use active verbs. "AI and Growth" is a label, not a theme. "Build AI systems that compound across sessions to create compounding productivity advantage" is a theme.
3. **Horizon balance**: One theme per horizon. An all-H1 theme set means you're executing without building or exploring options.

## Format

```
### YYYY Annual Themes

**Theme 1 (H1-dominant)**: [Active verb + specific territory + specific outcome]
*What this means for bets*: [which active bets express this theme]
*What we're explicitly NOT doing*: [the implied trade-off]

**Theme 2 (H2-dominant)**: ...

**Theme 3 (H3-dominant)**: ...

**Coherence check**: [Do all three themes express a single strategic logic, or are they independent?]
```
