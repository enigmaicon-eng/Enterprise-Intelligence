## Definition
Bets history is the compounding intelligence layer of the strategic system — the accumulated record of what was bet on, what happened, and what patterns were extracted. Unlike the operational `strategy/bet-log.md` (which tracks individual outcomes), this entry synthesizes patterns across multiple bets to build a higher-order model of strategic judgment quality over time.

## Why It Matters
A single bet postmortem is useful. A pattern across 10 postmortems is strategic intelligence. Tracking bets without tracking patterns across bets means learning stays local — each bet starts from scratch rather than benefiting from the accumulated judgment of previous ones.

## Meta-Patterns to Watch For

As the bet history accumulates, look for these second-order patterns:

**Horizon bias**: Are most bets H1? If so, the portfolio is execution-heavy and options-light. Sustained H1 dominance is a signal of strategic myopia.

**Kill condition quality**: Were kill conditions specific enough to trigger clearly, or were they always "ambiguous"? Systematic ambiguity means kill conditions were written to be unfalsifiable.

**Thesis retrospective accuracy**: When bets are reviewed 6 months later, how often was the original thesis right? A systematically overconfident thesis is a calibration problem.

**Cut vs. won ratio**: Neither extreme is obviously correct. A very high win rate may mean bets were too conservative (not really bets). A very low win rate may mean bets were poorly reasoned or theses never validated.

**Assumption quality**: Which class of assumptions (market, user behavior, technology, organizational) most often turns out to be wrong? This is a signal about where belief calibration is weakest.
