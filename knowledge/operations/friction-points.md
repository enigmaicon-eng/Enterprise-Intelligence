## Definition
Friction points are places where the workspace design creates unnecessary resistance — moments where doing the right thing requires more effort than doing the wrong thing, causing shortcuts, skipped steps, or workarounds. Tracking friction points is what makes the system self-correcting.

## Why It Matters
Unaddressed friction compounds. One skipped debrief is fine. Systematically skipped debriefs because the process is too slow means months of unprocessed meeting intelligence. Friction points predict where the compounding loop breaks before the break is visible.

## How to Add a Friction Point

When you notice resistance — something that should be fast but is slow, something you keep forgetting to do, a tool that requires too many steps — add an entry here:

```
### [Short Name]
**What**: What creates the resistance
**When**: The trigger context (what were you trying to do?)
**Frequency**: How often does this come up?
**Proposed Fix**: Specific change that would eliminate it
**Status**: observed | investigating | fixed | accepted
```
